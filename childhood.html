Childhood Roots

Meru Childhood, Chogoria Spirit

I was born in Meru on cool slopes filled with tea and eucalyptus. My days started with chores, then long rides to Chogoria Boarding Primary. At night we whispered about skating on real courts while the dorm guard made sure we stayed quiet. Meru taught me to create joy from scraps and to celebrate with everyone around me.

Family Influence

Mum ran a small shop near Giaki and Dad fixed boda headlights. They asked each of us to play an instrument and a sport, so we learned discipline through drums, harvest dances, and long errands.

Service Faith Resourcefulness

Community Lessons

On Saturdays we swept the Chogoria church path and then drew chalk lanes so we could skate. I led the assembly dances and turned Bible stories into movement.

Formative Moments

Story Circles Recorded aunties’ folktales on a small radio and retold them during prep time.
Science-Play Mashups Tracked rainfall on sugar sacks to win the Grade 5 science fair.
Dorm DJ Mixed gospel and urban songs for dawn warm-ups, which sparked my love for audio editing.
Roll Ball Discovery Teachers noticed my balance in PE and handed me a Roll Ball, changing the rest of my plans.

Lessons Carried Forward

  • New ideas can grow from scraps when the community helps.
  • Movement tells stories, so record every freestyle.
  • Leadership starts with chores and leads to wins.
