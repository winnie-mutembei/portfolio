Chapter 1

Meru Childhood, Chogoria Spirit

I was born in Meru, on slopes perfumed by eucalyptus and tea leaves. Days started with sunrise chores, then long matatu rides to Chogoria Boarding Primary. Nights were for whispering dreams about skating on real courts while the dorm guard checked our silence. Meru taught me to shape joy from limited materials and to share victories with the entire ridge.

Family Influence

Mum ran a duka near Giaki while Dad repaired boda headlights. They insisted that every child play an instrument and a sport, so my siblings and I learned discipline through drumbeats, harvest dances, and mile-long errands.

Service Faith Resourcefulness

Community Lessons

Saturdays meant sweeping the Chogoria church avenue, then turning the courtyard into a skate floor using chalk-drawn lanes. I became head choreographer for school assemblies, translating Bible stories into motion.

Formative Moments

Story Circles Recorded aunties’ folktales on a tiny radio cassette and retold them during dorm prep time.
Science-Play Mashups Tracked rainfall on sugar sacks to win the Grade 5 science fair.
Dorm DJ Mixed gospel and urban beats for dawn warm-ups, sparking my interest in audio editing.
Roll Ball Discovery Teachers spotted my skating balance during PE and handed me a borrowed Roll Ball, altering every plan I had.

Lessons Carried Forward

  • Innovation can be carved from scraps if community rallies together.
  • Movement is storytelling; document every freestyle.
  • Leadership is service—start with chores, end with championships.
